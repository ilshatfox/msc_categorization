.  SEQUENTUAL FIRST-CROSSING LOOK-AHEAD PROCEDURE FOR SELECTING A POPULATION WITH THE LARGEST MEAN IN NORMAL-NORMAL MODEL  I.A. Kareev  A problem of statistical selecting of population with the largest mean value is considered. We introduce a sequential selection procedure, which we call first-crossing look-ahead, for normal-normal Bayesian setting of the problem, where variances of the populations are supposed to be the same and known and the means are realizations of prior normal random variable with known distribution parameters. The paper includes the definition of the procedure with some basic analytical results.
