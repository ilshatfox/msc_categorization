Ovchinnikov suggested the hypothesis on connection of the “number” of interpolation spaces with the structure of the algebra of operators in a couple (family) of spaces. More precisely, in more spaces there are operators and the more conditions are imposed on the action of operators, the simpler is the structure of the algebra of operators. That is, in fact, the reason for the difference in the spaces constructed by different interpolation methods in families of more than two spaces from the case of a pair of spaces. The example, constructed in the paper, confirms this hypothesis to some extent. Keywords: operator algebras, families of Hilbert spaces, representation of operator algebras.
